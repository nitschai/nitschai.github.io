Ω Centauri

Omega Centauri (ω Cen, NGC5139) is the brightest, most massive globular cluster in the Milky Way and it has complex stellar populations. More specifically it shows multiple sequences in its color-magnitude diagram, large spread in metallicity and possibly also ages. Due to this, it has long be suspected to be a stripped of nuclei of a dwarf galaxy that has merged with the Milky Way a long time ago.

Abstract

In my work we investigate the formation history of ω Cen by mapping kinematics and metllicities of individual stars out to its half-light radius in a large MUSE mosaic. We are extracting a large number of stars (~ 110 000) with PampelMUSE (Kamann et al. 2013) and use the individually extracted stellar spectra to measure metallicities using spexxy and to estimate the ages. By connecting the spatial information to the kinematics and metallicities, we will be able to clearly identify member stars and get valuable information on its formation history.
